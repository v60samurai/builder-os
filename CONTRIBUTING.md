# Contributing to Builder OS

Thanks for considering a contribution. A few principles before you open a PR.

## What I'm looking for

- New templates that fill a clear gap (see the Roadmap in README.md).
- Filled-out examples of existing templates from real shipped products (redact what you must).
- Sharper, more opinionated writing in existing templates.
- Bug fixes: dead links, stale syntax, typos.

## What I'm not looking for

- Edits that water down the voice ("maybe consider perhaps possibly").
- Stack changes to the session playbook unless you ship a new variant alongside.
- Removing the confidence tags, "what we left out" sections, or any of the rigor-forcing scaffolding.
- AI-generated additions that read like slop. I will know.

## How to propose a new template

1. Open an issue first. Describe the problem the template solves, who needs it, and a 5-line outline.
2. If we agree it fits, send a PR with the template plus at least one filled-out example.
3. Match the existing voice: definite, concrete, opinionated. Tell readers what *good* looks like, not just what to fill in.

## Voice rules

- No em dashes. Use hyphens, colons, or commas.
- No corporate hedging. "It depends" is rarely the right answer.
- Define your terms. If a section uses a word like "anchor" or "surface," explain it where it first appears.
- Show, don't gesture. Concrete examples beat abstract advice.
